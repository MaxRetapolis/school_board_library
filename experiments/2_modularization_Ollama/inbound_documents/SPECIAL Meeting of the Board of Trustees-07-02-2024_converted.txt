NOTICE TO THE PUBLIC: Persons wishing to speak to the Board of Trustees regarding items on this agenda 
are requested to fill out a card to address the Board and adhere to the instructions therein. Individual speakers 
shall be allowed three (3) minutes to address the Board on each agenda item. The Board shall limit the total 
time for public input on each item to 30 minutes. With Board consent, the President may increase or decrease 
the time allowed for public presentation, depending on the topic and the number of persons wishing to be 
heard (Board Bylaw 9323).
In general, the Board recognizes that it is best practice to make important decisions before 10 p.m. and 
therefore will likely limit the time for public comment when there is a large number of speakers present for a 
particular agenda item. For example, if there are approximately ten (10) to fifteen (15) speakers on a single 
agenda item, the Board will likely limit the time to two (2) minutes per speaker. If there are more than sixteen 
(16) speakers on a single agenda item, then time for public comment will likely be limited to one (1) minute per 
speaker. Speaker slips must be turned in 1 minute after staff report for the item you are wishing to speak on.

Public Content
BACKGROUND:
Education Code section 5304 authorizes the Districts Board of Trustees to order an election of governing 
board members. Furthermore,Education Code section 5322 provides that whenever an election for governing 
board members is ordered, the governing board shall provide for specifications of the election order, which 
shall be delivered to the officer conducting the election.
To fulfill the requirements of Education Code 5322, each districts governing board, or an authorized officer 
thereof, must deliver specifications of the election order in the form of a resolution to the County 
Superintendent of Schools. This resolution shall include the authority for ordering the election, the date of the 
election, the purpose of the election and the signature of the officer or clerk of the board authorized to sign 
such resolution. Resolution 2024/25-03addresses these requirements, and is consistent with the by-trustee 
area election method adopted by Dublin Unified School District.
